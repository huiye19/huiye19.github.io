Hui Ye 叶卉

Assistant Professor • Hong Kong Bapist University

HKBU Profile Google Scholar LinkedIn ORCiD Email

I am a tenure-track Assistant Professor at the Department of Interactive Media, School of Communication, Hong Kong Baptist University. Previously I worked as an RGC Postdoctoral Fellow at the Division of Arts and Machine Creativity, the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST) and School of Creative Media, City University of Hong Kong (CityUHK). I obtained my PhD degree from CityUHK, under the supervision of Prof. Hongbo Fu. I received a BA in communication and a minor in computer science and technology from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), with Guo Moruo Scholarship honored.

Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of technical and empirical Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Computer Graphics (CG), and Communication & Media Studies, with a focus on content creation and interaction design with emerging technologies such as Generative AI and AR/VR. My work spans various practical and creative applications including 3D sketching, animation creation, game design and development, cultural heritage preservation, and artistic expression. My recent research explores:

  • Novel interactive techniques, tools, and systems with GenAI and AR/VR
  • How people utilize GenAI and LLMs in domain-specific creative applications
  • Technology-assisted artistic practice and creative works

I am actively looking for self-motivated PhD/Mphil students, research assistants, student helpers, and visiting students to join my group. Please refer to Group page for more details.

PhD/MPhil Students

The Hong Kong PhD Fellowship Scheme (HKPFS) application deadline is December 1 annually, while the regular RPg program deadline is March 1. Prospective students are encouraged to reach out early to discuss potential research directions and application strategies.

Research Assistants and Student Helpers

Part-time Research Assistants (RA) and Student Helpers are available for current undergraduate or master's students enrolled at Hong Kong universities. Full-time RA positions are open to all qualified candidates, with preference given to those with research experience or publications in related areas. Applicants planning to apply for PhD/MPhil programs in the future are particularly encouraged to apply.

Skills and Research Directions

Technical HCI & Graphics

Applicants with solid programming skills in Unity (C#), JavaScript, Python, Apple ARKit (Swift/Objective-C), or Google ARCore (Java/C++) are welcome to apply.

Empirical HCI

Applicants with experience in user study design, qualitative and quantitative research methods, statistical analysis, or mixed-methods approaches are encouraged to apply. Familiarity with tools such as R, SPSS, or qualitative coding software is a plus.

How to Apply

Please send your inquiry via email with the subject line: [Position Type]+[University]+[Your Name]

(e.g., "PhD+HKBU+John Smith" or "Student Helper+HKU+Mary Chan")

Email content should include:

  • A brief description of your research interests
  • The research direction(s) you would like to pursue
  • Your availability (start date and duration)

Attachments:

  • CV
  • Undergraduate (and master's, if applicable) transcripts
